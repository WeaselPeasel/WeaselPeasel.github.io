Facilities

School of Art Facilities

Green Hall

1156 Chapel Street

Digital Technology Office - Room 208

The digital technology office manages the Digital Labs throughout the School of Art. The Digital Labes contains Magintosh®-based facilities for undergraduate and graduate students from all areas of study. For more information click here.

Photography Lab

The photography lab contains darkrooms. The darkrooms are open to any undergraduate or graduate student currently enrolled in the Photography Department program of study. The photography lab also loans cameras out for intro classes. For more information click here.

Ralph Mayer Learning Center

The Ralph Mayer Learning Center is dedicated to Ralph Mayer, an author of many books about art. The center supports research and writing on the use of materials, and it is for studying the artists' techniques in the field of drawing and painting. For more information click here.

Printshop

353 Crown Street

Fabrication Shop

36 Edgewood

This building offers full wood and metal shops. These shops are open to graduate and undergraduate students currently enrolled in a sculpture course, after taking the appropriate training for thw wood and/or metal shop. For more information click here.

Yale Facilities

Yale University Art Gallery

1111 Chapel Street

"The mission of the Yale University Art Gallery is to encourage appreciation and understanding of art and its role in society through direct engagement with original works of art. The Gallery stimulates active learning about art and the creative process through research, teaching, and dialogue among communities of Yale students, faculty, artists, scholars, alumni, and the wider public. The Gallery organizes exhibitions and educational programs to offer enjoyment and encourage inquiry, while building and maintaining its collections in trust for future generations." To visit their website click here.

Yale Center for British Art

1080 Chapel Street

"The Yale center for British Art is a public art museum and research institute that houses the largest collection of British art oustide the United Kingdom. It was presented to the university by Paul Mellon (Yale College, Class of 1929), the collection reflects the development of British art and culture from the Elizabethan period onward. Admission is free." To visit their website click here.

Center for Collaborative Arts and Media

149 York Street

"The Center for Collaborative Arts and Media (CCAM) at Yale University is an experiment center for visual art, computer science, music, architecture, film and video, theater and dance, and literature. As a wing of Yale Arts, it does not provide a curriculum, but instead focuses on guiding students and visiting artists in expanding and exploring all manner of projects." To visit their website click here.

Yale Center for Engineering Innovation and Design

15 Prospect Street

"The Center for Engineering Innovation and Design serves as a hub for collaborative design and interdisciplinary activity at Yale University. Its goal is to enable the design, development, and actualization of ideas, from the whiteboard to the real world. Students, staff, and faculty from across Yale have access to CEID resources, participate in courses and events, and collaborate with CEID staff on a wide range of projects." To visit their website click here.

Yale Arts Library

180 York Street

The Yale Arts Library combines works on art from all of Yale's Libraries. On-site, the library has about 125,000 volumes, including rare and unique materials. Off-site, over 300,000 arts texts are stored in the Library Shelving Facility for delivery to any Yale Library. To visit their website click here.

Beinecke Rare Book & Manuscript Library

121 Wall Street

The Beinecke Rare Book & Manuscript Library is the rare book library and literary archive of the Yale University Libraries. It is one of the largest buildings in the world devoted to rare books and manuscripts. The collections contain archival material and works from present day. To visit their website click here.
